Disability Living Allowance (DLA) for children: Eligibility - GOV.UK
Eligibility criteria:

Child must be under 16
Need extra looking after or have walking difficulties
Be in England, Wales, an EEA country, or Switzerland when you claim (some exceptions apply)
Have lived in Great Britain for at least 6 of the last 12 months, if over 3 years old
Be habitually resident in the UK, Ireland, Isle of Man or the Channel Islands
Not be subject to immigration control

Special conditions:

Different rules apply in Northern Ireland
For children in Scotland, apply for Child Disability Payment instead
You can claim DLA for children if you're in or out of work

Non-British citizens:

EU, Swiss, Norwegian, Icelandic, or Liechtenstein citizens usually need settled or pre-settled status under the EU Settlement Scheme

Children under 3:

Under 6 months: must have lived in Great Britain for at least 13 weeks
6 months to 3 years: must have lived in Great Britain for at least 26 of the last 156 weeks
Rules may not apply if the child has 6 months or less to live

Disability or health condition requirements:

Child needs much more looking after than a child of the same age without a disability
Child has difficulty getting about
Difficulties must have lasted for at least 3 months and be expected to last for at least 6 months

Care component rates depend on the level of care needed:

Lowest rate: help for some of the day
Middle rate: frequent help or constant supervision during the day, supervision at night, or help while on dialysis
Highest rate: help or supervision throughout both day and night, or terminal illness

Mobility component rates depend on the level of help needed getting about:

Lowest rate (child must be 5 years or over): can walk but needs help and/or supervision when outdoors
Highest rate (child must be 3 years or over): cannot walk, can only walk short distances without severe discomfort, could become very ill if they try to walk, or are blind or severely sight impaired
